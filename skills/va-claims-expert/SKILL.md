## VA Math (Not Simple Addition!)

The combined rating uses this formula:
```
combined = A + B*(1 - A/100)
```
Where A is the higher rating, B is the lower.

Example: 60% + 30% = 60 + 30*0.4 = 60 + 12 = 72%

## Common Conditions & Ratings

### Heart Conditions (DC 7000-7010)
- 0% - Minimal, no medication
- 10% - Documented, no limiting
- 30% - Workload 4-6 METs, dyspnea
- 60% - 2-3 METs, dyspnea, fatigue
- 100% - CHF, severe symptoms

### Diabetes (DCs 7913, 7919)
- 10% - Diet-controlled
- 20% - Oral medication
- 40% - Insulin + diet

### Spine (DCs 5235-5242)
- 10% - Limited motion
- 20% - Muscle spasm, lost motion
- 30% - Unfavorable ankylosis
- 50% - Intervertebral disc syndrome

### Mental Health (DCs 9400-9440)
- 0% - Minimally symptomatic
- 30% -Occupational, reduced reliability
- 50% - Reduced reliability, intermittent
- 70% - occupational, difficulty
- 100% - Total occupation and social

## TDIU Requirements
- One condition at 60%
- OR Combined 70%+ with one at 40%+

## PACT Act (38 CFR 3.114)
- 1-year lookback for toxic exposures
- Presumptive conditions for Burn Pits, Agent Orange, etc.

## When User Asks About VA Rating:

1. Gather all medical evidence
2. Identify service-connected conditions
3. Calculate combined rating using VA Math
4. Determine potential back pay
5. Consider TDIU eligibility

## Key Questions to Ask:
- What conditions have been diagnosed?
- When did symptoms start?
- How do conditions affect employment?
- What medical evidence exists?

## Red Flags in Claims:
- No nexus letter linking to service
- Missing medical evidence
- Not claiming secondary conditions
- Not claiming TDIU when eligible
